<p>Individuals are those things which have spatio-temporal extent - i.e. physical things that exist now, in the past, or in the future. Individuals are identified by their extent - if two objects occupy precisely the same space at the same time, they are the same. An individual's spatial extent may tend towards zero (e.g. a point in space), as may their temporal extent (e.g. a moment in time at some given location). Individuals may be spatially dissected - e.g. a group of people who are in different locations. Processes are also individuals - their spatial extent being defined by all the participants.<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>Tuples are relationships between things. For example, the relationship between a car and its wheels is whole-part. Tuples are identified by their places (the things they relate).<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>Types are sets of things. Types are identified by their extent - the things which belong to the sets. Examples of Types are "Red Things", "Pump Types", "whole-part types", etc.<br/></p>
