<p>A Property that is always exhibited by its instances (Individuals). Formally, a CategoricalProperty is the set of things that have a property simpliciter without reference to the capability to manifest another property (as is the case with DispositionalProperties (qv.)). So, for example, the property of 'flying at Mach 2' is a  CategoricalProperty, whereas 'being capable of flying at Mach 2' is not. <br/></p><p><br/></p><p>Examples:<br/></p><p><br/></p><p>10kg<br/></p><p>40cm<br/></p><p>Fitting in an ISO container<br/></p>
